# Float — getting it on your iPhone

Two files matter: **float.html** (the whole app) and **icon.png** (optional home-screen icon).
Your thoughts are stored **only on your phone**, never online — with either option.

---

## Option A: GitHub Pages (recommended — most reliable storage)

One-time, ~5 minutes, free.

1. Go to **github.com** in any browser and create a free account (skip if you have one).
2. Tap **+** → **New repository**. Name it anything neutral, e.g. `notes-app`. Keep it **Public**. Create.
3. On the repo page: **Add file → Upload files**. Upload `float.html` and `icon.png`. Commit.
4. **Important:** rename `float.html` to `index.html` (open the file → pencil icon → change the name → commit). Or just rename it before uploading.
5. Repo **Settings → Pages** → under "Branch" pick `main` and `/ (root)` → Save.
6. Wait ~2 minutes, then open `https://YOURUSERNAME.github.io/notes-app/` **in Safari on your iPhone**.
7. Tap the **Share** button → **Add to Home Screen** → Add.

Done. It now opens full-screen like a real app, works offline after first load, and iOS treats its data as app data (very stable).

> The page itself is public, but it's an empty shell — nothing you write ever leaves your phone.

## Option B: Local file only (nothing online at all)

1. Send `float.html` to your iPhone (AirDrop, email to yourself, or iCloud Drive).
2. Save it into the **Files** app.
3. Tap it in Files — it opens in a viewer/Safari and works right away.

**Caveat:** iOS is allowed to clear browser data for files opened this way, especially if storage runs low. So:

- Once a week (right before therapy is a natural moment), open **More → Copy backup** and paste it into Apple Notes or an email to yourself.
- If entries ever vanish: **More → Restore**, paste your last backup. Nothing else is lost.

Honestly, Option A + the weekly backup habit is the safest combo.

---

## Weekly therapy routine

1. Before your session: **Week** tab → **Build summary** → **📄 Share PDF** (AirDrop, WhatsApp, email…) or **Save PDF**. A plain-text version is there too if you prefer copy-paste.
2. The report has counts, average loudness, a thoughts-per-day chart, time-of-day patterns, and every entry with timestamps — hand it over or read it out. See `sample-report.pdf` for what it looks like.
3. After the session: **More → Copy backup** into Notes. 30 seconds, and your data is safe forever.

## A note on how to use Float well

- Jot the thought **once**, let it float, go back to what you were doing. That's the whole skill.
- Try not to reread entries during the week — the Week tab is for session day.
- If you notice urges to rewrite a thought until it "feels right" or to check the app for reassurance, that's worth mentioning to your therapist (it can be a compulsion pattern).
- The app deliberately has **no streaks, no goals, no reminders**. Missing days is fine. Catching a thought late still counts.
